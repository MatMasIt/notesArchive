$$
(\sqrt{2}-1)^x+(\sqrt{2}+1)\leq 2\\
$$
Razionalizzo
$$
(\sqrt{2}+1)\frac{(\sqrt{2}-1)}{(\sqrt{2}-1)}=\frac{2-1}{(\sqrt{2}-1)}\\
\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}-1}
$$
Risolvo
$$
(\sqrt{2}-1)^x+\frac{1}{(\sqrt{2}-1)^x}-2\leq0\\
\frac{(\sqrt{2}-1)^{2x}-2(\sqrt{2}-1)^x+1}{(\sqrt{2}-1)^x}
$$
Regola dei segni
$$
N\geq0\\
(\sqrt{2}-1)^{2x}
$$
